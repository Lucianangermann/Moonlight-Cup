# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Badminton tournament organizers (primarily one person) running a live tournament in a gym. They manage the app from a tablet or laptop at courtside; the screen is also projected live so players and spectators can follow the tournament state. The user is standing, potentially moving around, and needs to act quickly between matches. Every interaction happens under mild time pressure with ambient noise and low light.

## Product Purpose

MoonlightCup manages the full lifecycle of a badminton tournament evening: participant registration, automated round-robin matchup generation, real-time score entry, leaderboard tracking, and match timers. Success means the organizer can run a smooth tournament without paper, and players can glance at the projected screen to understand standings at a distance.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Intense · Night. Authoritative without arrogance. The design should feel like a well-run competition — controlled energy, not chaotic excitement. It earns trust by being crisp and legible under real conditions.

## Anti-references

- Gaming/neon aesthetics: RGB glow colors, Fortnite-style UI, excessive light effects. The night theme is literal (gym at night) not a trend aesthetic.
- Generic sports app mass-market UI (ESPN, FIFA apps) — too loud, too red/green, too consumer.

## Design Principles

1. **Legible at projection distance.** Any number, name, or status shown on screen must be readable from 3–4 meters. This overrules density decisions.
2. **Gold is authority, not decoration.** The accent color signals "this is the active / important thing." It must not be diluted by broad use. When everything is gold, nothing is.
3. **Hierarchy through scale, not effects.** Size jumps carry meaning. A score should look dramatically different from a label. Borders, shadows, and gradients are not a substitute for clear scale contrast.
4. **Night-native dark.** Dark mode is not a preference toggle — it is the only context this product runs in. Design for a dim gym at 10pm, not a bright office.
5. **Calm intensity.** The design should convey a competition in progress, not a loading screen or a marketing page. Information density is high, but the reading path is always clear.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Projection use means contrast requirements are stricter in practice than on paper — aim for 7:1 on primary text. Reduced motion support required (tournament timers and state updates should not rely on animation to convey information).
